# A structure's health bar divides by its BASE HP, not the power-degraded hitpointsMax

**Finding:** a structure has two maxima. `StructureInfo[type].o.hitpoints` is the **base** (full) HP. `Structure.hitpointsMax` is the **power-degraded** cap: `Structure_CalculateHitpointsMax` (`structure.c:623`) rescales it each house tick to `base * powerRatio / 256` (floored at `base/2`), and when that drops below current HP the structure bleeds 1 HP/tick toward it. So an under-powered structure legitimately has `o.hitpoints > hitpointsMax` for a while (e.g. an outpost placed at its full 500, then `hitpointsMax` recomputed to 250 under a power deficit, bleeding down). The **health bar denominator is the base**, not the degraded cap: OpenDUNE draws `GUI_DrawProgressbar(o->hitpoints, si->o.hitpoints)` (`widget_draw.c:725`, `oi = &si->o`). Dividing by the degraded `hitpointsMax` instead shows an over-full / wrong-length bar and a nonsensical readout like `400/250`.

**Why it matters:** `FrameInfo.Structure.hitpointsMax` and the inspector readout are **display** values — they must mirror what OpenDUNE's HUD divides by (the base), even though the engine's internal `Structure.hitpointsMax` is the degraded cap. Units don't have this split (their max never degrades), so only structures need the base-HP denominator. The degraded cap is still the engine's real cap (repair heals only up to it, degradation pulls HP toward it) — it's just not the *display* max.

**Evidence:** `Frameworks/DuneIISimulation/FrameInfo+Make.swift` (structures emit `hitpointsMax: StructureInfo[type].o.hitpoints`); `Apps/duneii/GameModel.swift` `currentInfo` (inspector max = base); the degraded cap lives in `GameState+Lifecycle.swift` `structureCalculateHitpointsMax`. Test: `FrameInfoTests.structure` (windtrap with a degraded `hitpointsMax` of 120 and current HP 175 still reports base max 200).

**How to apply:** any HP-bar / "x / max" readout for a structure divides by `StructureInfo[type].o.hitpoints`. Reserve `Structure.hitpointsMax` for engine logic (repair ceiling, degradation target), never for the displayed maximum.
